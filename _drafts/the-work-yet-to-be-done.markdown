George Scialabba was a cradle Catholic [who became a Rortian](https://yalebooks.yale.edu/book/9780300289633/the-sealed-envelope/). I read Rorty in my late 20s, my first round of grad school in the late 90s. I was very influenced by *Achieving Our Country* and only decades later was ready to understand the critique of Alisdair MacIntyre and others whose arguments for moral foundationalism were part of how I became Catholic. I didn't become Catholic and a foundationalist because *liberalism just needs a coherent house to hold it all together*. I got there only with a lot of internal resistance, with a midlife intellectual bottleneck that I couldn't resolve.
